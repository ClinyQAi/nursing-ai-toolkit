# Nursing Examples (Assessment)

:::info Attribution
**Original work**: "Educators' guide to multimodal learning and Generative AI" — Tünde Varga-Atkins, Samuel Saunders, et al. (2024/25) — [CC BY-NC 4.0](https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc/4.0/)  
**Adapted for UK Nursing Education by**: Lincoln Gombedza, RN (LD)  
**Last Updated**: December 2025
:::

This prompt library is designed to help **educators** create robust assessment materials and rubrics.

## 1. Rubric Generation
Create detailed marking criteria in seconds.

**Prompt**:
> "Create a marking rubric for a Level 5 (Year 2) Nursing Essay on 'Service Improvement'.
> The 4 grading bands should be: Fail, Pass (40-59), Merit (60-69), Distinction (70+).
> The 3 criteria are:
> 1. Evidence of critical analysis.
> 2. Application of change management theory.
> 3. Feasibility of the proposed improvement.
> **Output as a table.**"

## 2. Multiple Choice Question (MCQ) Bank
Rapidly generate knowledge checks for VLEs (Moodle/Blackboard).

**Prompt**:
> "Create 5 Multiple Choice Questions on 'Diabetes Management'.
> Target audience: Year 1 Nursing Students.
> Difficulty: Progressive (Q1 is easy, Q5 is hard).
> For each question, provide 4 options (A, B, C, D) and identify the correct answer with a short rationale."

## 3. Generating "Model Answers"
Help students understand what "Good" looks like by generating an exemplar.

**Prompt**:
> "Write a single paragraph 'Model Answer' for the following exam question:
> *'Discuss the role of the nurse in safeguarding vulnerable adults.'*
>
> The answer should demonstrate 'Distinction' level writing: concise, evidence-based, and citing the Care Act 2014.
> *Note: This will be used to show students the expected standard.*"

## 4. Assessment Brief Clarification
Check if your assignment instructions are clear.

**Prompt**:
> "Act as a confused Year 1 student with English as a second language.
> Read this assignment brief I have written: [Paste Brief].
> Tell me 3 parts of this brief that are ambiguous or hard to understand. Ask me clarifying questions."

:::tip Quality Assurance
Always review the generated rubrics and questions to ensure they align with your specific module learning outcomes. AI helps with the *format*, you provide the *standard*.
:::
